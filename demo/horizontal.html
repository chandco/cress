Welcome to CRESS

Corporate Responsibility for the Environment, Society & Sustainability

Being a responsible corporate citizen is immensely important to us. That’s why we have developed a detailed strategy to guide the business, ensuring that we are considerate to the environment within which we operate, the people with whom we engage, and the legacy our business practices will leave.

Our CRESS programme summarises our approach and inspires the business to keep setting new standards in responsible working.

Our 2012 CRESS report details our achievements to date and targets for this year. We will be providing annual updates on the progress we have made.

Our Mission.

At CH&Co it is our mission to minimise our impact on the environment, provide an inspiring and safe place to work, contribute towards making the world a better place, and play an active role in ensuring the industry within which we work develops with a sustainable long term vision.

To guide our approach our CRESS programme sets out clear objectives which fall under five key areas particularly close to our hearts:

  • Health and Wellbeing
    Supporting the wellbeing of our customers and staff through healthy eating initiatives and by raising awareness of the value of good nutrition, are both at the heart of our organisation.
  • People
    We are passionate about developing our people and helping them build successful careers with us.
  • Suppliers and Sourcing
    We always strive for excellence in the way we source our ingredients and products and work closely with our suppliers to create a fair and sustainable supply chain.
  • Environment
    We care about the environment and work hard to minimise the impact we have in all that we do as a business
  • Community
    We enjoy being able to give back to and support those within the wider community and society in which we live and work.

The Environment

Our Goal:

To minimise our impact on the environment in all that we do

We continually strive to improve our environmental performance and work hard to ensure we comply with all applicable legislation. Our success in doing this is reflected in our achievement of the ISO 14001:2004 standard across the business.

Our Head Office and certain sites were accredited the ISO14001 standard on 29th February 2009. The business as a whole was accredited at the end of 2011.

Our Environmental Objectives are

  • To reduce the amount of non-recyclable disposable good used by units
  • Units to work with their Clients to encourage recycling and other “green” initiatives at a local level
  • To work with CH&Co approved suppliers to encourage supply-chain traceability and the procurement of sustainable and fair-trade food and drink items whilst not entailing excessive costs

We have set targets and performance levels which are regularly monitored to assist in achieving our objectives. These objectives are set out in our CH&Co Environmental Management System which all of our units work to.

All of our new starters receive environmental training as part of our Discover training programme.

How we monitor how we are doing

All of our units and sites have a completed a Environmental Aspects and Impacts Risk Assessment. Control measures are put into place to reduce the impact each site has on the environment. Our unit managers also complete a biannual unit environmental review to ensure compliance.

The Environment

Recycling of used Cooking OiL

CH&Co purchased approximately 3,500 barrels of oil in the last six months. 2,026 of the barrels were returned with waste oil (58%). All waste oil is collected by the producer, refined and converted to biofuel. This is then used to run the machinery on the rape seed farm.

Reducing Emissions

Car Fleet
The CH&Co car fleet is selected with reduced CO2 emissions in mind. Our fleet currently consists primarily of BMW and Mini Cooper vehicles as these perform particularly well in terms of CO2 emissions compared to other models on the market.

Deliveries
Our supplier 3663 currently deliver grocery products, frozen products, confectionary, and janitorial products, as well as chemicals (Ecolab products) and coffee (Paddy & Scott and vending) to our units. By utilising the 3663 vehicles to deliver other suppliers’ products, we are reducing the number of deliveries made to sites and in turn reducing the CO2 emissions.

Packaging
We currently purchase 336 packaging lines from Tri-Star, of which 163 lines are made from recycled materials (48.5% of purchases). Only one product from the 336 lines is not recyclable (99.7% of products are recyclable). And of the 336 lines, 100 are compostable – all of the compostable items are recyclable too, equating to 29.7%.

Suppliers & Sourcing

Our Goal:

To work with our suppliers to create a fair and sustainable supply chain

We are committed to working with our suppliers to create a supply chain which is built on a principle of sustainable and, wherever possible, local sourcing.

our Longest supplier relationship is with West Horsley Dairies (who have supplied us for 20 years).

Working with Our Suppliers

  • We look for long term relationships – fixed term contracts where everyone knows the rules and both parties need to play their part
  • We establish a level playing field for each category when re-tendering
  • We allow openness across a category – for example all meat suppliers are aware of our top 10 product shopping basket prices from their competitors, which are all shared with our sites.
  • We have become a preferred business partner in many cases, and we have never sought extended credit from any of our suppliers
  • All or our suppliers are vetted for their CSR credentials and their products are reviewed in line with our policies

 

Fair-trade food

  • 82% of all our coffee, tea, and sugar is fair-trade or Rainforest Alliance accredited
  • We us 100% fair-trade or Rainforest Alliance certified coffee from both fresh-brew suppliers
  • Our vending sugar is all Tate & Lyle fair-trade

 

Red Tractor

We are licensed users of this widely-recognised food mark, which confers confidence about the provenance of food. Red Tractor guarantees 100% British source, focus on widely accepted standards of animal husbandry, concern for the natural environment, and methods of horticulture.

Our Red Tractor Facts

  • 100% of our chicken fillets are Red Tractor certified
  • 100% of our 1%-fat milk (our default milk product) is Red Tractor certified
  • 85% of our meat is Red Tractor certified
  • 45% of our fruit and vegetables are red tractor certified – this is subject to the vagaries of the British weather which can have severe effects on fruit and vegetable availability.

 

Marine Stewardship Council (MSC)

We are currently working with the MSC towards full membership

  • All our wet fish suppliers are MSC accredited already
  • There is a direct link between the fishermen and the end user – assuring a fair price and sustainable approach
  • Complete Chain of Custody information is provided with each delivery, so we can pass this information on to our customers – right down to the skipper/boat name
  • We encourage the use of less known and less popular fish varieties such as in our “Ugly Fish Friday” campaign

 

Ugly Fish Friday Campaign Details
Our commitment to sustainable sourcing is shown through initiatives such as the Ugly Fish Friday campaign, which sees us bringing sustainable but often unknown fish species into our restaurants for our chefs to cook. This campaign has been hugely successful in highlighting the need to use fish from sustainable sources as well as promoting just how delicious these little known species are.

Local Sourcing

  1. We always look to balance between price, availability, delivery terms, and impact on the environment / local employment

 

  1. We encourage our sites to reduce the number of deliveries they have per week to cut down delivery miles
  2. We combine small suppliers under the strength of a larger one to help reduce overall food miles
    • For example Marshfield Icecream (Wiltshire) distribute via the strength of Stratford Fine Foods
    • Fairfield Crisps (Norfolk) distribute via the strength of DDC Foods
    • Speciality cheeses (from all over the UK) distribute via West Horsley Dairies and Oliver Kaye
    Maintenance of choice and competition between our suppliers for our chefs has always been of paramount importance – no one-drop deliveries for us!

Community Fact: Marys Meals

Our corporate charity in 2011/12 was Marys Meals, a global movement that sets up school feeding projects in some of the world’s poorest communities where poverty and hunger prevent children from getting an education.

They provide one daily meal in a place of learning to attract poor children into a classroom where they can gain an education. Through its fundraising initiatives CH&Co provided 183,333 meals for Marys Meals. In addition donations of school backpacks at the CH&Co annual conference in 2011 provided 190 schoolbags for children studying with the Marys Meals organisation.
